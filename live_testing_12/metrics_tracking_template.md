# Lead Generation Metrics Tracking Template

Track your lead generation performance to understand what's working and optimize your strategy.

## Daily Tracking Spreadsheet

Create a spreadsheet (Google Sheets or Excel) with these columns:

| Date | Platform | Engagements | Responses | Clicks | Leads | Time Spent | Notes |
|------|----------|-------------|-----------|--------|-------|------------|-------|
| 2024-01-15 | Reddit | 5 | 2 | 3 | 1 | 30min | Good response in r/LangChain |
| 2024-01-15 | Twitter | 8 | 3 | 1 | 0 | 20min | Thread got good engagement |
| 2024-01-15 | GitHub | 2 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 15min | Helped with issue #123 |

### Column Definitions

- **Engagements**: Comments, replies, posts you made
- **Responses**: People who replied to you
- **Clicks**: Website clicks (use UTM tracking)
- **Leads**: People who showed interest/signed up
- **Time Spent**: How long you spent on this platform
- **Notes**: What worked, what didn't, ideas for next time

## Conversation Quality Scoring

Rate each conversation (1-5) on:

**Relevance (1-5):**
- 5: Perfect fit, actively building AI agents
- 4: Building something related, interested
- 3: Tangentially related
- 2: Somewhat interested
- 1: Not relevant

**Engagement (1-5):**
- 5: Multi-message conversation, asked questions
- 4: Thoughtful response, showed interest
- 3: Brief positive response
- 2: Acknowledgment only
- 1: No response

**Intent (1-5):**
- 5: Ready to try product now
- 4: Interested, wants to learn more
- 3: Curious, might try later
- 2: Just gathering info
- 1: No clear intent

**Track in spreadsheet:**

| Date | Platform | User | Relevance | Engagement | Intent | Total | Notes |
|------|----------|------|-----------|------------|--------|-------|-------|
| 1/15 | Reddit | user123 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 13 | Asked about integration |
| 1/15 | Twitter | @dev456 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 9 | Liked the tip |

**Quality Score = Relevance + Engagement + Intent** (max 15)

- **12-15**: High quality lead, follow up
- **8-11**: Medium quality, stay engaged
- **4-7**: Low quality, minimal follow-up
- **1-3**: Not a lead

## A/B Testing Tracker

Test different approaches and measure results:

### Test 1: Response Style

**Hypothesis:** More casual tone gets better engagement on Reddit

**Test A (Formal):**
- Sample size: 20 responses
- Response rate: 30%
- Conversation depth: 2.5 messages avg

**Test B (Casual):**
- Sample size: 20 responses
- Response rate: 45%
- Conversation depth: 3.8 messages avg

**Winner:** Test B (casual tone)
**Action:** Adopt casual tone for Reddit

### Test 2: Content Type

**Hypothesis:** Tutorials perform better than case studies

**Tutorials:**
- Published: 3
- Avg views: 2,000
- Avg clicks: 120
- CTR: 6%

**Case Studies:**
- Published: 2
- Avg views: 1,500
- Avg clicks: 90
- CTR: 6%

**Winner:** Tutorials (higher reach, same CTR)
**Action:** Focus on tutorials

## Weekly Review Questions

Ask yourself these every week:

**Effectiveness:**
1. Which platform drove the most quality leads this week?
2. What type of content/engagement worked best?
3. What was my cost per lead (time invested)?

**Efficiency:**
1. Where did I waste time this week?
2. What could be automated better?
3. What should I stop doing?

**Growth:**
1. What new tactic did I try?
2. What did I learn about my audience?
3. What opportunity did I discover?

**Quality:**
1. How helpful were my responses (1-10)?
2. Did I build any lasting relationships?
3. What feedback did I receive?

## Remember

**Measure what matters:**
- Quality > Quantity
- Conversations > Clicks
- Relationships > Reach
- Learning > Likes

**Iterate based on data:**
- Review weekly
- Adjust monthly
- Pivot quarterly

**Stay consistent:**
- Small daily actions compound
- Track everything to see patterns
- Celebrate small wins

---

Start simple. Track the basics. Add complexity as you learn what matters most for your specific audience and product.
